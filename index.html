What is the Dining Philosophers Problem?

Five philosophers sit around a circular table. Between each pair of philosophers is a single chopstick. To eat, a philosopher needs BOTH chopsticks (left and right). The challenge: how do they share resources without creating deadlock or starvation?

Key Concepts:

  • Concurrent Access: Multiple processes trying to access the same resource simultaneously
  • Deadlock: When all philosophers hold one chopstick and wait forever for the other
  • Starvation: When a philosopher never gets to eat
  • Resource Hierarchy: A strategy to prevent deadlock by ordering resources

C10 Comparison Rules:

  • Thinking and eating times follow discrete uniform distribution [1, 10]
  • All philosophers start by thinking
  • Hungry philosopher always picks up LEFT chopstick first, then RIGHT
  • In simultaneous access: philosopher on the RIGHT gets priority
  • Simulation terminates when deadlock is detected
